I’m PhD student in Biostatistics at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Dr. Wei Xu. Before that, I completed my Master in statistics at the University of Saskatchewan, supervised by Dr. Longhai Li, followed by a 2-year research associate in Dalla Lana School of Public health at the University of Toronto and Princess Margaret Cancer Centre. My research interests are in statistical genetics, causal inference, high-dimensional data, and survival analysis. Besides research, I would spend my time on painting, piano, cooking, and photography. My philosophy of life and research is “Stay Hungry. Stay foolish.”
